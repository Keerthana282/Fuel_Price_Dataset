# ⛽ A Data-Driven Analysis of Fuel Price Trends and Volatility in Argentina

---

## 📌 Project Overview
Fuel prices in Argentina fluctuate significantly across **time**, **fuel types**, and **regions**, creating uncertainty for consumers, transportation businesses, and policymakers.  

This project applies **Python-based data analytics techniques** to transform raw fuel price data into **actionable insights** that support informed decision-making.  

The analysis follows a complete analytics lifecycle including **descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics**, making it a strong real-world data analytics case study.

---

## ❓ Problem Statement
Fuel price volatility in Argentina raises key questions:
- Which fuel types are the most expensive or volatile?
- How do fuel prices change over time?
- Do certain regions consistently experience higher prices?
- What are the economic implications of fuel price fluctuations?

This project aims to analyze historical fuel price data to address these challenges and support data-driven decisions.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives
- Analyze historical fuel price trends in Argentina  
- Identify regional variations in fuel prices  
- Compare pricing differences across fuel types  
- Measure fuel price volatility and fluctuation patterns  
- Detect periods of significant price increases or decreases  
- Improve understanding of economic impacts related to fuel price changes  

---

## 📊 Analytics Approach

### 🔹 Descriptive Analysis
Provides an overview of fuel pricing behavior across time, regions, and product categories.

**Key Findings**
- Fuel prices have increased steadily over time  
- Premium fuels consistently cost more than regular fuels  
- Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) has the lowest average price  
- Day and night shift prices show minimal variation  

---

### 🔹 Diagnostic Analysis
Explains the underlying factors influencing fuel price variations.

**Key Questions Addressed**
- Why are premium fuels more expensive?
- Why do certain regions experience higher fuel prices?
- Why is CNG consumption relatively low despite its lower price?

**Key Drivers Identified**
- Fuel quality and refinement levels  
- Regional logistics and transportation costs  
- Economic conditions and infrastructure availability  

---

### 🔹 Predictive Analysis
Uses historical trends to estimate future fuel pricing behavior.

**Predicted Insights**
- Fuel prices are likely to continue increasing if current trends persist  
- Premium fuels will remain the most expensive fuel category  
- Regional price gaps may widen under sustained inflationary pressure  

---

### 🔹 Prescriptive Analysis
Translates analytical insights into actionable recommendations.

**Recommendations**
1. **Focus on Cost-Effective Fuels**  
   Promote affordable fuels such as **CNG** in high-price regions to reduce consumer burden and transportation costs.  

2. **Monitor Inflation and Volatility**  
   Policymakers should closely track fuel price inflation and introduce stabilization or subsidy mechanisms when necessary.  

3. **Regional Pricing Strategy**  
   Fuel companies can optimize supply chains and improve distribution efficiency in high-price regions to reduce logistics costs and regional disparities.  

4. **Premium Product Positioning**  
   Premium fuels should be marketed to specific customer segments where demand justifies higher pricing.
